## Context

The trading system framework currently executes trades based on signals generated by strategies. However, there is no centralized, standardized mechanism to enforce risk limits (such as stop-loss or take-profit) on active positions. These rules must be applied robustly and independently of the primary trading strategy to ensure capital protection.

## Goals / Non-Goals

**Goals:**
- **Centralized Risk Management**: Implement a `RiskManager` component that evaluates active positions against defined risk criteria.
- **Pluggable Strategies**: Provide a `BaseRiskStrategy` interface so developers can create various risk strategies (e.g., fixed percentage, trailing stop).
- **Event-Driven Integration**: Ensure the `RiskManager` subscribes to market events and position updates, and emits risk signals (e.g., `RiskSignalEvent`) when thresholds are breached.
- **Execution Engine Coupling**: Update the execution flow to intercept or act upon risk signals to immediately close positions.

**Non-Goals:**
- **Complex Portfolio-Level Risk**: Initial focus is on per-position risk (stop-loss/take-profit), not complex portfolio risk parity or correlation-based limits.
- **Pre-trade margin checks**: This is handled by the account service, not the post-trade risk management module.

## Decisions

### 1. RiskManager as an Event Listener
The `RiskManager` will register for `EventType.MARKET` (to evaluate price changes) and `EventType.FILL` (to track active positions).
- **Rationale**: This decouples risk management from the main strategy logic. It operates in parallel, constantly evaluating risk without interfering with the primary strategy's signal generation.
- **Alternatives**: Embedding risk logic inside `StrategyManager`. *Decision rationale*: Separation of concerns. Risk should be an independent layer of defense.

### 2. Pluggable Risk Strategies
Risk rules will be implemented as classes inheriting from `BaseRiskStrategy`. The `RiskManager` will hold a list of active risk strategies for each position or globally.
- **Rationale**: Allows high flexibility. Users can plug in different risk rules without modifying the core `RiskManager`.
- **Alternatives**: Hardcoding stop-loss percentages. *Decision rationale*: Too rigid for a generic framework.

### 3. Dedicated Risk Signal Event
When a risk threshold is hit, the `RiskManager` will emit a `RiskSignalEvent`.
- **Rationale**: The `ExecutionEngine` (or a coordinator) can specifically listen for risk signals, which might be prioritized over regular strategy signals to ensure immediate closure.
- **Alternatives**: Emitting a standard `SignalEvent`. *Decision rationale*: A dedicated event type allows the system to distinguish between a strategy's decision and a forced risk liquidation, which can be useful for logging and priority routing.

## Risks / Trade-offs

- **[Risk] Conflicting Signals** → The primary strategy and the risk manager might generate signals simultaneously. **Mitigation**: The execution engine should prioritize `RiskSignalEvent` over standard `SignalEvent` for the same symbol.
- **[Risk] Latency** → Evaluating multiple risk strategies on every tick for every position could introduce latency. **Mitigation**: Optimize the evaluation loop and consider evaluating risk primarily on bar updates unless high-frequency ticking is strictly required.
